EULER'S METHOD FORMULA
----------------------

Given: dy/dt = f(t,y)

Approximation: y(n+1) = y(n) + h * f(t(n), y(n))

For Newton's Cooling:
  f(t,T) = -k * (T - T_room)
  
  T(n+1) = T(n) + h * [-k * (T(n) - T_room)]
  T(n+1) = T(n) - h * k * (T(n) - T_room)

With our values (k=0.07, T_room=20):
  T(n+1) = T(n) - 0.07*h * (T(n) - 20)


ACCURACY ORDER
--------------

Euler's method is a first-order method:
  Global error ∝ h

Halving the step size approximately halves the final error:
  h=10 → h=5:  Error 0.072 → 0.058  (Factor: 1.24)
  h=5  → h=2:  Error 0.058 → 0.030  (Factor: 1.93)

(Factors not exactly 2.0 due to problem-specific behavior)


COMPUTATIONAL EFFICIENCY
------------------------

Accuracy × Efficiency Trade-off:

  h=2s:  51 steps → 0.030°C error → 0.00059°C/step
  h=5s:  21 steps → 0.058°C error → 0.00276°C/step
  h=10s: 11 steps → 0.072°C error → 0.00655°C/step

For this problem, h=2s provides best overall value:
  - Excellent accuracy
  - Still very fast (< 1 second)
  - Only 51 steps total


PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION
-----------------------

The cooling follows an exponential decay curve:
  
  T(t) = 20 + 80 * exp(-0.07*t)

At t=0:   T = 20 + 80 = 100°C (initial)
At t=100: T = 20 + 80*e⁻⁷ ≈ 20.07°C (nearly room temp)

Half-life: t₁/₂ = ln(2)/k = 0.693/0.07 ≈ 9.9 seconds

After 100 seconds (≈10 half-lives), the temperature difference
has reduced to (1/2)¹⁰ ≈ 1/1024 of the original.
